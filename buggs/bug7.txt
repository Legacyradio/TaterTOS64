TaterTOS64v3 - bug7 — SYS_SBRK partial-allocation rollback on failure
========================================================================

TIMESTAMP
---------
2026-03-04 11:28:22 CST

WHY THIS LOG
------------
- Reported medium-severity heap-growth bug in `SYS_SBRK`.
- On mid-loop OOM or mapping failure, previously allocated pages could remain
  mapped even though the syscall returned failure.

BUG
---
- Paths reported:
  - `src/kernel/proc/syscall.c:508`
  - `src/kernel/proc/syscall.c:510`
  - `src/kernel/mm/vmm.c:149`
- Root issue:
  - `SYS_SBRK` allocated/mapped pages in a loop.
  - If allocation failed after some pages were already mapped, syscall returned
    `-1` without undoing prior mappings.
- Impact:
  - partial heap growth despite failure return.
  - retry behavior could leak pages or collide with stale mappings.

FIX APPLIED
-----------
1) Added rollback helper for heap-growth attempt
- File: `src/kernel/proc/syscall.c`
- New helper `sbrk_rollback_pages(...)`:
  - scans growth range
  - translates user VA->PA in target CR3
  - unmaps mapped pages
  - frees corresponding physical pages

2) Hardened SYS_SBRK failure handling
- File: `src/kernel/proc/syscall.c`
- Added guards:
  - overflow and user-top bounds checks for `new_end/new_page_end`
  - pre-existing mapping detection in growth range
- Added rollback on:
  - `pmm_alloc_page()` failure
  - failed/mismatched mapping result after `vmm_map_user`
- Result: all pages mapped during failed growth attempt are rolled back before
  returning `-1`.

3) Added user-space VA translation helper in VMM
- Files: `src/kernel/mm/vmm.h`, `src/kernel/mm/vmm.c`
- New API:
  - `uint64_t vmm_virt_to_phys_user(uint64_t pml4_phys, uint64_t virt)`
- Used by `SYS_SBRK` rollback logic and mapping verification.

WHY THIS FIX
------------
- Preserves syscall atomicity expectation for growth attempts: either full
  requested page-range growth succeeds or no new mappings remain.
- Eliminates stale partial mappings after OOM/mid-loop failure.

FILES CHANGED

- src/kernel/mm/vmm.h
- src/kernel/mm/vmm.c
