fry398 — Intel PCR[DMI] LPC IOE mirror + EC early-status diagnostics
Timestamp: 2026-02-28 22:38:52 UTC

PROBLEM:
  On Dell Precision 7530 (Intel 300-series PCH), EC still failed at 0x62/0x66
  with sts=0xFF even after restoring default EC ports. LPC config IOE looked
  enabled, but sideband decode likely was not mirrored to PCR[DMI], so EC I/O
  cycles were not forwarded.

CHANGES:
1) src/kernel/acpi/extended.c
   - Added Intel SBREG/PCR constants and PCR diagnostic globals.
   - Added local inb() helper and early EC status probe in ec_reg_init():
     g_ec_early_sts = inb(0x66) before any EC init work.
   - Fixed IOE bitmask bug in ec_enable_lpc_decode():
     - old: bit 13 (EC 0x4E/0x4F)
     - new: bit 11 EC_62_66 + bit 10 KBC_60_64
   - Implemented pcr_get_sbreg_bar() to unhide/read/re-hide P2SB (00:1f.1).
   - Implemented ec_mirror_pcr_ioe() to mirror IOE and IOD into PCR[DMI]
     registers (LPCIOE/LPCIOD) via physmap UC mapping.
   - Wired PCR mirror call after Intel LPC IOE programming.
   - Exposed new diagnostics in acpi_get_diag().

2) src/kernel/acpi/extended.h
   - Extended struct fry_acpi_diag with:
     pcr_ioe_before, pcr_ioe_after, pcr_mirror_done, p2sb_hidden, ec_early_sts.

3) src/user/libc/libc.h
   - Mirrored the same fry_acpi_diag struct extension for userspace ABI sync.

4) src/user/apps/sysinfo.c
   - Increased window height (430 -> 480).
   - Added PCR diagnostic line:
     "PCR IOE: ... mir=... early=..."
   - Shifted subsequent battery/backlight lines down by 16 px to avoid overlap.

WHY:
  Intel eSPI/LPC decode on affected platforms may require PCR[DMI] mirror writes
  in addition to PCI config IOE writes. The new path keeps Intel gating checks,
  preserves non-Intel behavior, and surfaces enough diagnostics to determine if
  PCR mirror resolved sts=0xFF.
