TaterTOS64v3 - fry526 — Preserve FAT32 secondary candidates when NTFS exists
========================================================================

TIMESTAMP
---------
2026-03-04 17:33:21 CST

WHY THIS LOG
------------
- User reported: apps launch in QEMU but not on Dell Precision 7530.
- Root cause path still existed in VFS secondary mount policy.

ROOT CAUSE
----------
- `vfs_mount_secondary()` treated FAT32 candidates with app signatures as
  "boot media" and suppressed all FAT32 candidates whenever a non-FAT32
  partition (e.g., NTFS) was present.
- On real hardware with mixed partition types, this could hide app-bearing
  FAT32 volumes from `/nvme*` mountpoints, making app launch appear broken.
- QEMU could still appear fine because ramdisk payloads were available.

FIX APPLIED
-----------
- File: `src/kernel/fs/vfs.c`
- Function: `vfs_mount_secondary(...)`
- Changed policy:
  - Removed FAT32 candidate suppression in the `boot_media_seen && non_fat32_seen`
    case.
  - Keep FAT32 candidates mountable even when NTFS/ToTFS alternatives exist.
  - Added explicit diagnostic log:
    `VFS: boot-media-like FAT32 seen at %s with non-FAT32 alternatives; keeping FAT32 candidates`
