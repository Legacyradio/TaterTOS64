- User-reported bare-metal boot stall on Dell Precision 7530 after:

WHY
---
- Early kernel serial output used an unbounded COM1 transmit-ready spin.
- On systems without a usable UART path, first early print (e.g. `K_START`)
  can hang forever and look like an ExitBootServices lockup.
- Fix required hardware-agnostic behavior: detect UART availability at runtime
  and fail open (continue boot) when serial is unavailable.

CHANGE APPLIED
--------------
- Updated `src/boot/early_serial.h`:
  - Added one-time UART presence probe using loopback test.
  - Added cached serial state (unknown/present/unavailable).
  - Made `early_serial_putc()` bounded with timeout.
  - Suppressed early serial writes when UART is unavailable.
- No global Dell-only assumptions were introduced.
