CONTEXT
-------
- Review flagged that command timeouts did not trigger controller reset/recovery.

WHY
---
- Timeout implies queue/controller may be wedged; bounded retry alone is
  insufficient without a reset/reinit path.

CHANGE APPLIED
--------------
- File updated: `src/drivers/storage/nvme.c`
  - Added per-device timeout/recovery flags:
    - `cmd_wait_timed_out`
    - `resetting_controller`
  - Added helpers/prototypes:
    - `nvme_reinit_after_timeout()`
    - forward declaration for `nvme_init_from_mmio()`
  - Timeout detection wiring:
    - `wait_cq()` now clears `cmd_wait_timed_out` at entry.
    - `poll_cq()` sets `cmd_wait_timed_out=1` on timeout exit.
    - `wait_cq_irq()` sets `cmd_wait_timed_out=1` on timeout exit.
  - `submit_cmd()` now:
    - detects timeout-specific wait failures,
    - emits timeout trace (`cmd_timeout`),
    - invokes guarded controller reset/reinit path.
  - `nvme_reinit_after_timeout()` performs:
    - guard against recursive reset,
    - MMIO phys derivation from current mapping,
    - controller shutdown + buffer free,
    - full `nvme_init_from_mmio(mmio_phys)` reinit,
    - ready-state clear + trace on failure.

EFFECT
------
- Timeouts now have explicit controller recovery behavior instead of silent
  fail-only path.
- Reset recursion is prevented by per-device guard.
