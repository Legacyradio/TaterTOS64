CONTEXT
-------
- Review flagged brittle physmap coverage logic using `+ 1` and `phys + len` in
  range-mapping loops, with risk of first/last page ambiguity depending on
  helper semantics.

WHY
---
- `vmm_ensure_physmap[_uc](phys_end)` maps the 2 MiB chunk containing
  `phys_end - 1` (end-exclusive contract).
- Using `phys + off + 1` plus final `phys + len` can work, but is fragile and
  easy to reason about incorrectly at boundaries.

CHANGES APPLIED
---------------
- Files updated:
  - `src/drivers/storage/vmd.c`
    - Reworked `vmd_ensure_mapped(phys, len)` to compute explicit first/last
      covered 2 MiB chunks for `[phys, phys + len - 1]`.
    - Maps each chunk via end-exclusive calls (`base + 2 MiB`) and adds
      overflow guards.
  - `src/kernel/acpi/tables.c`
    - Reworked `acpi_ensure_mapped(phys, len)` with the same explicit chunk
      coverage pattern and overflow-safe end computation.

EFFECT
------
- Removes ambiguous off-by-one behavior in both VMD CFGBAR and ACPI SDT mapping
  paths.
- Guarantees first and last covered chunks are mapped for non-zero range
  lengths, matching helper contract directly.
