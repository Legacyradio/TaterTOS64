WiFi TLV parse rc=-2 fix: wrong magic constant + off-by-one TLV type IDs

Problem:
  `ws` on Dell showed: FAIL @ tlv-parse (step 4) rc=-2
  Firmware loading (step 3) now works (fry620/621 embedded blob fix),
  but TLV parsing failed immediately at magic validation.

Root cause (iwlwifi_fw.h):
  1. IWL_UCODE_TLV_MAGIC was 0x5A4B3C2D — WRONG.
     The real Intel iwlwifi TLV magic is 0x0a4c5749 ("IWL\n" in LE).
     The embedded firmware blob has 0x0a4c5749 at offset 4 (correct),
     but iwlwifi_fw_load() couldn't match it → magic_offset stayed 0xFFFFFFFF
     → iwlwifi_fw_parse_tlv() returned -2 ("no TLV magic found").

  2. TLV type IDs were off-by-one for gen2 sections:
       SEC_RT   was 18 (collided with FLAGS=18) — should be 19
       SEC_INIT was 19 — should be 20
       SEC_WOWLAN was 20 — should be 21
     Per Linux kernel iwl-fw-file.h canonical values.

Fix (src/drivers/net/iwlwifi_fw.h):
  - Changed IWL_UCODE_TLV_MAGIC from 0x5A4B3C2D to 0x0a4c5749
  - Changed SEC_RT from 18 to 19
  - Changed SEC_INIT from 19 to 20
  - Changed SEC_WOWLAN from 20 to 21

Verified: firmware header parses correctly:
  offset 0:  0x00000000 (zero word)
  offset 4:  0x0a4c5749 (magic)
  offset 8:  "release/core43::7e3e4b69" (human_readable)
  offset 72: 0x0000002e (API version 46)
  offset 88: first TLV type=0x16 (DEF_CALIB), len=0x0c
